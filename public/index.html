Introduction




Code After Hours is a organization that was founded by the idea of having Coding Clubs in Puerto Rico schools. Founded and being maintained by two High School students and a Psychologist/Web Designer. The principal goal of Code After Hours is for providing hgih school students resources, help and mentors so they can implement coding clubs at their Schools. Then the main organizer of the school becomes the mentor for that school, this way everyone's learning together. Every week Organizers from different school will have a meeting to discuss teaching topics and the progress of each club. Through the school year, Code After Hours will be having monthly Hackathons in which the students will participate and apply what they have learn in their clubs, this will be a practice for the anually Hackathon in which the students have a chance to win prizes.

About



We have discovered that in Puerto Rico, even when the public school system has invested so much money in technology for schools, they're not teaching it the right way. The public school system has been teaching students how to be users, not creators.


What did we do about it? We created Code After Hours, an idea on how to it better. We mentor young people to start their own programming clubs. While doing this they're not just learning how to code, they're learning leadership, team work, good values, and much more to discover.
